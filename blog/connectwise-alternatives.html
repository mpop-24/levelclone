8 Best ConnectWise Alternatives in 2024 (Free & Paid Options)

8 Best ConnectWise Alternatives in 2024 (Free & Paid Options). 1. Level 2. Datto RMM 3. Splashtop 4. ManageEngine RMM Central 5. NinjaOne

Level

Wednesday, November 27, 2024

ConnectWise is a popular IT management solution that offers powerful tools for remote monitoring, automation, and data security.

However, as your operations evolve, it's important to look for software that better aligns with your company’s unique needs, budget, and goals.

This article lists the best ConnectWise alternatives and competitors to consider for your organization. It also includes tips on how to choose the right platform.

Advantages of ConnectWise

ConnectWise is an all-in-one IT management platform that helps IT teams deliver fast, flexible, and secure remote monitoring. It offers the following benefits:

Source: ConnectWise.com

Unified Platform

ConnectWise integrates several IT management functions into a single platform. It combines remote access capabilities with remote monitoring and management (RMM) as well as professional services automation (PSA).

ConnectWise also provides cybersecurity and data protection tools to safeguard remote devices against cyberattacks.

Robust Remote Access Features

Speaking of remote access, ConnectWise is known for its ScreenConnect software. This platform provides powerful remote control capabilities that enable IT teams to manage systems from any location.

ScreenConnect also features screen sharing, file transfer, and session recording to support clients and end users. These tools can quickly address issues and maintain system uptime.

User-Friendly Interface

ConnectWise has an intuitive interface that is easy to use. It allows IT staff to access important tools and information without unnecessary complexity. Meanwhile, clients and other end users can easily navigate the platform.

This user-friendly design can also minimize the learning curve and increase IT efficiency.

Customization Options

ConnectWise's remote management platform offers extensive customization. This enables IT teams to tailor workflows, dashboards, and reports to their specific needs.

It also ensures that the tool aligns with specific business processes and improves operational efficiency.

Scalable Management Features

ConnectWise is designed to scale with your organization. It doesn't matter if you're managing a small team or a large enterprise.

ConnectWise quickly adapts to various business sizes and complexities. It can continue to meet your evolving IT management needs.

Limitations of ConnectWise

While ConnectWise offers numerous advantages, it's important to consider that it also has some limitations. Let us take a closer look at each one below:

Steep Learning Curve for New Users

ConnectWise's extensive features can be a double-edged sword. Yes, they can be useful for companies handling complex IT environments. However, they can also overwhelm new users or small internal teams.

The platform's complexity may require significant time and training for staff to become proficient. It can delay the full implementation of the software.

Extra Costs for Additional Features

While ConnectWise provides an advanced set of tools, some advanced features come at an additional cost.

For example, the platform offers sound capture to enable direct communication with clients or end users. However, this feature limits you to either hear the customer or speak with them. There is no option to do both without upgrading your subscription.

These extra expenses can add up, especially for smaller IT departments with limited budgets.

Connectivity Issues

ConnectWise faces occasional connectivity problems during remote sessions.

For instance, some users report that the platform randomly disconnects from a linked endpoint, such as mobile devices or servers. This makes device management more complicated than it needs to be.

Many customers also experience that ConnectWise suffers from slow performance when they don't have a stable internet connection.

These disruptions can hinder support efforts and lead to frustration for both IT staff and end users.

Frequent Bugs

On top of connectivity issues, users have also encountered bugs that affected ConnectWise's performance.

These glitches can slow down workflows and lead to unintended mistakes. Troubleshooting such issues requires time, which can disrupt business operations.

Delayed Responses from Customer Support

Although ConnectWise offers different support options (phone, live chat, email), the responsiveness of its technical support team is slow.

It takes a lot of effort to receive proper answers from ConnectWise. Some users have to resort to figuring out the problem on their own.

Such delayed responses or poor customer assistance can be frustrating, especially when your IT team deals with important issues.

Splashtop's features are limited to remote access and control. The platform doesn't offer automated software provisioning, advanced reporting, or extensive asset inventory.

Splashtop is also resource-intensive since it requires software installation on the local desktop and the client side.

Plus, it includes extensive customization options. It lets organizations tailor the RustDesk client with their brand logos, colors, and icons.

Criteria for Choosing the Best ConnectWise Alternatives

If you're still unsure which alternative to pick, you can consider the following features below:

RMM Capabilities

ConnectWise is known for its remote monitoring and management features. When searching for top alternatives, you must evaluate their RMM capabilities.

Check if they provide real-time insights into each endpoint's status, health, and usage, among key performance indicators.

You should also verify that these tools offer unattended access to multiple devices.

The better the RMM platform, the more you can focus on strategic projects instead of manually handling technical issues.

Customization Options

Personalized dashboards, customizable workflows, and flexible alerts are key features to consider when comparing different ConnectWise alternatives.

These customization options allow you to tailor the platform to your unique processes. They can increase staff productivity because IT teams no longer need to adapt to the platform. Instead, the software can match your department's requirements.

Automated Tools

You should look for automated patch management, scripting, and ticketing. With these automated tools, you can significantly lighten the workload for your IT team.

You can free up valuable resources to focus on strategic initiatives that drive business growth. At the same time, you can minimize the risk of human error.

Security and Compliance Standards

Security is a non-negotiable feature when searching for the best ConnectWise competitors.

Consider solutions that use encrypted connections, multi-factor authentication, and custom access permissions.

You must also check if the vendor meets security standards set by regulatory bodies.

When you prioritize security, you can keep your sensitive information confidential. You can also build trust with more clients, which can directly impact your revenue.

Platform Compatibility

You must choose a tool that integrates seamlessly with your existing tech stack.

For example, if your IT teams manage Linux endpoints and mobile devices, check if the software offers multi-platform support.

It's best to invest in a solution that is compatible with multiple operating systems, servers, and devices.

You should also consider if it has built-in integrations with popular third-party apps. These may include PSA software, ticketing systems, and documentation tools.

Pricing

The cost of software is always an important consideration for businesses.

Compare different ConnectWise alternatives not only in terms of pricing but also with regard to their return on investment (ROI).

You should specifically look for transparent pricing models that match your organization’s size and needs. Make sure it delivers value for your money without compromising key features.

You must avoid tools with hidden fees or features locked behind expensive paywalls.

Customer Support

Finally, you must consider the quality of support you'll get from the software vendor.

Choose a tool with responsive support teams, detailed documentation, and active user communities. These can ensure help is available when you face issues with the platform. At the same time, they can maintain uptime and team productivity.
