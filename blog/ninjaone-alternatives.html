6 Best NinjaOne Alternatives for IT Teams

6 Best NinjaOne Alternatives for IT Teams. 1. Level 2. Datto 3. N-able 4. Action1 5. GoTo Resolve 6. ConnectWise Automate

Level

Monday, September 30, 2024

As an IT manager, you recognize the importance of remote monitoring and management (RMM) tools that increase productivity and solve business challenges.

NinjaOne is one of the platforms for monitoring online systems and managing your IT infrastructure proactively.

However, you may seek alternatives to NinjaOne to find solutions that better fit your budget, specific feature requirements, or integration capabilities.

In this article, we will explore other NinjaOne alternatives for better remote monitoring and management.

What is NinjaOne?

Source: NinjaOne

NinjaOne is an IT management software that supports IT teams by providing tools for device monitoring, patch management, secure remote access, and more.

It features a user-friendly interface that helps improve operational productivity. It also reduces workloads through automation and centralized control.

What Are the Limitations of NinjaOne?

Although NinjaOne offers many benefits, it has certain limitations. These may urge IT teams to consider other remote monitoring platforms.

Let's take a look at the downsides of NinjaOne.

Steep Learning Curve

New users find NinjaOne quite daunting to use. While the software has robust features and interface, it’s not as intuitive, especially for new IT hires.

This steep learning curve can lead to prolonged training periods. NinjaOne may delay the time it takes for IT teams to become fully operational and proficient with the RMM software.

Difficult to Manage and Set Up

NinjaOne is a robust RMM software that gives you access to various tools and features. Unfortunately, it can be difficult to manage and set up. 

For example, rarely used settings are challenging to find in the dashboard. Sometimes, they are hidden under other menu functions. Thus, it may take time to configure systems, which results in decreased productivity.

Complex Integration with Other Software Systems

Integration capabilities are important for smooth IT operations, especially when your team uses a diverse set of tools and platforms. 

Integrating NinjaOne with less common software and custom in-house applications can be challenging. Instead of increasing efficiency, this can lead to additional work for IT professionals.

It constrains them to provide custom solutions to bridge the gap between different programs. 

Overall, NinjaOne’s limited integration abilities may be a deal breaker for IT teams relying on a diverse stack of technologies.

Expensive Software

Cost is a significant factor for any business. NinjaOne's pricing structure can be a barrier for small to medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) or startups. It may become too costly as organizations scale and more endpoints are added.

Additionally, transparent pricing is not shown on the website. The only way to find it is by requesting a quote.

These factors make NinjaOne less appealing compared to more cost-effective alternatives.

Key Factors to Consider When Looking for NinjaOne Competitors

If you think NinjaOne isn't a good tool for your IT needs, you can find many alternatives on the market. To choose the right one for your business, you must consider certain features, such as:

Automation and Remote Management Capabilities

A key benefit of any RMM software is its ability to automate repetitive tasks. This reduces the need for manual intervention and frees up your IT team for higher-value work.

Look for tools that handle patch management, updates, and monitoring without needing constant oversight.

They can simplify time-consuming processes, which can minimize human error and boost efficiency across your organization.

A good RMM tool should also enable robust remote management. This allows your IT team to resolve issues quickly without being physically present.

Ease of Use

Your team’s productivity depends on how quickly they can adopt a new tool.

Avoid complex dashboards that take weeks to master and slow down IT operations. Instead, choose RMM tools with a user-friendly and intuitive interface to reduce the learning curve.

Customization Options

Every IT team operates differently, so having the ability to customize the RMM tool is essential.

Some platforms offer rigid templates, while others let you tailor dashboards, alerts, and reports to fit your specific needs.

The more control you have over these features, the easier it is to optimize the tool for your team’s workflow.

Security and Compliance

With cybersecurity being a top concern for IT leaders, choosing an RMM tool with strong security features is non-negotiable.

Look for NinjaOne alternatives that offer data encryption, role-based access control, and compliance with industry standards.

Integration Capabilities

Choose an RMM tool that integrates seamlessly with your existing technology stack. This includes ticketing systems, security solutions, and cloud platforms.

Strong integration capabilities reduce friction between systems and improve productivity.

Cost and Licensing Structure

RMM pricing is another important consideration, especially for IT teams working with tight budgets.

Different RMM tools offer various pricing models, from per-device licensing to fixed monthly fees. Make sure the total software cost aligns with your team’s needs and budget.

Customer Support

No matter how reliable the RMM software is, issues are bound to arise. When they do, having access to a responsive customer support team can make all the difference.

Look for software providers that offer 24/7 support, live chat, or direct access to technicians who can resolve issues promptly.

Scalability

As your IT infrastructure grows, your RMM tool needs to evolve with it.

Choose a solution that can scale effortlessly as you add more devices, users, or locations. This ensures that you won’t need to switch tools as your organization expands.

Action1 stands out for its patch management features and automated remediation capabilities. It helps IT professionals discover, prioritize, and resolve real-time system issues as they arise.

These include remote computer monitoring, ticketing alerts, automated workstations, and mobile device management.

GoTo Resolve is easier and faster to learn compared to NinjaOne, which has a relatively steep learning curve. This makes the platform appealing to small and medium-sized businesses.

On the downside, GoToResolve is not as comprehensive as other remote monitoring and management software solutions in this list.

Its limited features may not be for those seeking a robust ticketing support system, reporting data, and patch management.

Pricing

GoTo Resolve offers flexible pricing options depending on your business needs.

The cheapest plan, Remote Access, charges $23 per month when billed annually. However, it only allows access to 25 devices.

Meanwhile, the Premium plan's pricing varies depending on your specific needs.

6. ConnectWise Automate

ConnectWise Automate is a robust RMM solution designed to improve IT management through automation and out-of-the-box scripts.

It empowers IT teams to automate repetitive tasks, improve endpoint management, and achieve greater visibility across their infrastructure.

With seamless integration into the ConnectWise Sidekick platform, Automate also creates PowerShell scripts in minutes. This allows your IT department to focus on delivering proactive support and building stronger client relationships.

Despite these advantages, ConnectWise Automate can be difficult to use for beginners. It has a clunky user interface that makes it challenging to navigate different features.

Pricing

ConnectWise Automate does not publicly post pricing details for their RMM software. To get accurate pricing, you must request a custom quote based on your team's unique needs and the number of endpoints to be managed.

FAQs About NinjaOne Alternatives

What should IT managers look for in NinjaOne competitors?

IT managers should prioritize RMM tools that offer comprehensive monitoring and management features, support for various devices, integration capabilities with other systems, and cost-effectiveness.

What is remote monitoring and management, and why is it important for IT teams?

Remote monitoring and management tools allow IT teams to monitor and control devices, networks, and endpoints remotely. This helps improve efficiency by automating tasks, reducing downtime, and proactively addressing issues before they escalate.

What does fixed price mean in the context of RMM tools?

A fixed price model in RMM tools refers to a pricing strategy where the cost is predetermined based on certain parameters, such as per endpoint or per user, regardless of the amount of actual usage.
