6 Best N-able Alternatives in 2024 (Free + Paid)

6 Best N-able Alternatives in 2024 (Free & Paid Options). 1. Level 2. ManageEngine 3. Datto 4. GoTo Resolve 5. Domotz 6. Veeam

Level

Monday, December 2, 2024

N-able combines advanced remote monitoring and management (RMM), backup, endpoint security, and disaster recovery features in one platform. It is a solid choice for managing IT assets within your organization.

However, you might seek a specialized tool that better meets your needs, budget, or integration requirements.

In this article, you will learn about the top N-able alternatives. You'll also find out the criteria to consider when choosing the best IT management platform.

Advantages of N-able

N-able is a popular platform for remote monitoring and management and IT service management (ITSM). It brings the following benefits for managed service providers (MSPs) and internal IT teams:

Source: N-able.com

Extensive IT Management Capabilities

N-able offers multiple IT management solutions that help business leaders monitor, manage, and secure their entire IT infrastructure from a single platform. Let's break down each software below:

  • N-able N-central: N-able offers N-central, one of two RMM tools. It is designed for large organizations or MSPs that manage multiple clients' network devices.
  • N-able N-sight: This RMM software is suitable for small businesses and IT teams. It offers a complete remote management solution for as low as $99 per month.
  • Cove Data Protection: N-able also provides backup and recovery solutions that allow you to retrieve important data in just a few seconds.
  • Endpoint Security: N-able's platform can also close security gaps through proactive system monitoring, threat investigation, and network security protection.
  • MSP Manager: It offers a ticketing system, a billing tracker, and a reporting tool that make it easier for MSPs to deliver better customer service.

User-Friendly Interface

N-able's IT management software is known for its intuitive interface. It is easy to navigate the dashboard where you can remotely monitor and manage assets.

Having a user-friendly design makes daily asset management less of a headache. It frees your IT department to focus on high-priority tasks rather than wasting time navigating a complicated interface.

N-able can also minimize the learning curve and allow new employees to get up to speed faster, which can lead to higher productivity.

Advanced Automation Features

N-able automatically handles routine tasks, such as patch management, software deployment, and network discovery.

It allows you to automate activities on a schedule or trigger them when certain conditions are hit. For example, let's say you own an automated patch management system.

Once it detects new bug fixes from software suppliers, it can instantly download and deploy them across your platforms.

This task automation can save valuable time, especially for IT teams managing large infrastructures or multiple operating systems. It reduces manual data entry and human error.

Scalable Platform

N-able offers a flexible solution that adapts as your IT environment expands. It can handle various devices, locations, users, and departments without compromising performance.

As your organization grows, N-able allows you to add more endpoints and extend your management reach seamlessly.

However, it can also scale down just as easily if your organization requires it.

Integration Capabilities

N-able supports integration with several third-party tools. These include IT automation platforms, endpoint management systems, and professional service automation software, among other apps.

Seamless integration reduces the need to switch between different platforms. It can connect your IT ecosystem and transfer data smoothly.

Limitations of N-able

While N-able offers many benefits, it has certain limitations that may discourage business leaders from investing. Here are the common shortcomings of this IT solution:

Slow Customer Support

Although N-able is easy to use, some IT teams may still have questions regarding the platform. To address their concerns, they often contact the software's customer support team.

Unfortunately, N-able is known for having slow and unresponsive remote support. It takes a long time to reach specialists who can answer questions or suggest resolutions to problems.

Slow response times can lead to extended downtimes and disrupted operations, which can result in lost revenue.

Limited Customization

Another downside of N-able is its limited customization options. You may think that it allows you to tailor its different capabilities to your needs. However, that's not the case at all.

For example, while N-Able offers dashboards and reporting tools, the options to customize these for specific metrics or views can be restrictive.

So if your IT environment requires a high degree of customization, N-able might hinder your ability to create workflows aligned with your needs.

Risk of Alert Fatigue

N-able uses automated monitoring tools to detect system slowdowns or potential security threats. Even though they are helpful, they can potentially lead to alert fatigue.

Many users report that the platform generates a high volume of alerts. These include notifications for minor issues that may not require immediate attention.

For IT teams managing large numbers of endpoints, this constant stream of alerts can quickly become overwhelming. It can also increase the risk of missing important reminders because of the noise from less important notifications.

Performance and Stability Concerns

N-able occasionally faces performance and stability issues, especially when handling larger environments or more complex networks.

Some professionals struggle accessing the site properly due to the slow response times, delayed data loading, and system crashes.

These problems can add complexity to daily tasks. For one, the IT department might not be able to complete tasks punctually during time-sensitive situations.

What to Look for in N-able Competitors?

If you're still struggling to choose the right N-able alternative, you can consider the following factors below.

Remote Monitoring and Management Capabilities

The best N-Able alternative should offer robust RMM capabilities. These include remote device access, real-time monitoring, and proactive alerting for potential issues.

You must look for a tool that provides an extensive view of your IT network. This will allow you to monitor your assets' status, health, and usage.

You should also check if the platform is compatible with your operating systems or devices. For example, some RMM software platforms only support Windows and Mac OS and not Linux endpoints.

Automation Features

Task automation capabilities are important for any IT management solution.

Choose a platform that can handle routine tasks such as system checks, data backups, and patch deployment.

Tools with built-in templates can save time because they don't require IT teams to write code. They can instantly deploy tasks based on pre-defined thresholds.

Meanwhile, scripting tools allow you to create custom automated workflows. For example, they can chain multiple actions together to accomplish several tasks across multiple environments. This significantly reduces manual workload.

Security and Compliance Features

Security is non-negotiable in IT management because you oversee sensitive company data and resources.

That's why you should select an N-able alternative with strong security measures. Check if it supports complex passwords, multi-factor authentication, encryption, and role-based access controls.

You must also verify that the platform complies with security and privacy requirements. This guarantees the confidentiality of your network information.

Audit logs, compliance reporting, and data backups are also game-changers in security. These features add an extra layer of protection against cyberattacks.

Ease of Use

You must also prioritize N-able alternatives with an intuitive interface, customizable dashboards, and logical workflows.

Look for drag-and-drop functionality, clear labeling, searchable menus, and filtering options. These features save time and reduce frustration for teams.

Choosing an easy-to-use platform can also minimize the need for training. It can help new team members to get up to speed quickly.

Pricing

Cost is always an important consideration when comparing different N-able competitors.

You should select solutions that align with your budget and offer flexibility as your organization grows. As much as possible, seek vendors with transparent pricing models.

Avoid platforms with hidden fees or lock-in contracts that limit your ability to adapt.

Customer Support

Lastly, you must check if the platform offers responsive customer support.

Can you reach support through chat, email, phone, or all of the listed options? Will you be able to talk directly with the software specialists or entry-level customer care?

You should also consider extensive documentation, training videos, and an active community forum. These resources can help your IT department quickly resolve issues without contacting the support team.
